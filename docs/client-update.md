# EduWave – Client Guide (How to use the app)

This guide explains how to use EduWave step-by-step. The screens you see depend on your role (Facilitator/Nanny, Trainer/Supervisor, Organisation Admin, or Super Admin).

## 1) Getting started
### Step 1: Open the app
1. Open the EduWave app from your phone.
2. If this is your first time opening it, allow notifications only if you want reminders (optional).

### Step 2: Sign in
1. Tap “Sign In”.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Tap “Sign In” again.

If your login does not work:
- Double-check the email spelling.
- Make sure there are no spaces at the start or end of the email/password.
- If you recently changed the password, fully close the app and open it again, then retry.

## 2) First-time setup (Accessibility Mode)
To make the app comfortable for different needs, you may be asked to choose an accessibility mode.

1. Select the option that feels best for you (for example, easier reading or higher contrast).
2. Continue to the app.

You can change this later in settings.

## 3) How the app is organised
The app is grouped into simple sections. Depending on your role, you may see:
- Training / Training Hub
- Check-ins
- Supervision
- Resources (Directory + Map)
- Journey (your progress view)
- Organisation Overview (read-only)
- Admin (user management, permissions)

## 4) Training (LearnWorlds link-based)
EduWave does not host training videos or lessons. It gives you a clear checklist and opens training in LearnWorlds (external).

### For Facilitator/Nanny: complete assigned modules
1. Open “Training” or “Training Hub”.
2. You will see courses grouped by level (Level 1, Level 2, etc.).
3. Open a course to see the modules.
4. To view training content:
   - Tap “Open in LearnWorlds”.
5. After you finish a module:
   - Tap the module and mark it completed.
6. The status updates immediately and the completion date is saved.

### For Trainer/Supervisor: assign training to a facilitator
1. Open the Training admin/assignment section (Trainer view).
2. Choose the facilitator.
3. Select the course level and modules.
4. Assign the modules.
5. The facilitator will see them in their Training screen.

### How to review progress (Trainer/Supervisor)
1. Open the facilitator’s training progress screen.
2. Review which modules are:
   - Not started
   - In progress
   - Completed

## 5) Check-ins (short support check-ins)
Check-ins are quick entries to understand how support is going and what is needed next.

### For Facilitator/Nanny: submit a check-in
1. Open “Check-ins”.
2. Answer the short questions (confidence, emotional load, support needed, notes).
3. Tap “Save” or “Submit”.

### For Trainer/Supervisor: review check-ins
1. Open the check-ins list for your facilitators.
2. Review trends over time.
3. Use check-ins to decide if supervision or follow-up is required.

## 6) Supervision (logs + follow-ups)
Supervision helps track coaching/support sessions and ensures action items are not missed.

### For Trainer/Supervisor: create a supervision log
1. Open “Supervision”.
2. Select the facilitator.
3. Add session notes and key outcomes.
4. If actions are needed:
   - Add a follow-up and set what should happen next.
5. Save the supervision log.

### For Facilitator/Nanny: view supervision follow-ups (if available)
1. Open “Supervision” (or Journey).
2. View the follow-up items and notes.

## 7) Resources (Directory + Map)
Resources help staff locate support services quickly.

### View resources in a list
1. Open “Resources”.
2. Browse the directory list.
3. Open a resource to see details (contact, address, notes).

### View resources on the map
1. From “Resources”, open the map view.
2. If asked, allow location access to show nearby services.
3. Tap a map pin to view details.

If you prefer not to share location, you can still use the list view.

## 8) Journey (progress overview)
Journey is a simple overview of your progress across training, check-ins, and follow-ups.

### For Facilitator/Nanny
1. Open “Journey”.
2. Review your current items:
   - Training module statuses
   - Recent check-ins
   - Any follow-ups

## 9) Organisation Overview (read-only)
This view is designed for Organisation Admins. It provides a summary view without exposing personal details.

### For Organisation Admin
1. Open “Organisation Overview”.
2. Review the summary:
   - Check-in totals and averages
   - Supervision totals and follow-up totals
   - Training completion totals

## 10) Admin functions (Super Admin)
Super Admin has the highest level access.

### Manage users and roles
1. Open “Admin”.
2. View users and roles.
3. Update roles or assign organisation (if required).

### Remove a person safely
1. Open “Admin”.
2. Find the person (child/facilitator/etc.).
3. Choose remove/delete.

This removal is safe and does not break the system.

### Give delete permission to another admin
1. Open “Admin”.
2. Select the admin user.
3. Grant permission for safe deletion actions.

## 11) Recommended walkthrough (for a full demo)
Use this flow to understand the complete system:
1. Trainer assigns training modules to a facilitator.
2. Facilitator opens LearnWorlds training and marks modules complete.
3. Facilitator submits a check-in.
4. Trainer creates a supervision log and a follow-up.
5. Organisation Admin reviews the overview summary.

## 12) Troubleshooting (simple)
### Login says “Invalid credentials”
- Confirm you are using the correct email and password.
- Try typing the password again carefully.
- Fully close the app and open it again.
- If the password was changed recently, contact the admin to confirm the latest login.

### The map does not show nearby resources
- Allow location access when prompted, or use the list view instead.

### A section is missing
- Some screens are only visible for certain roles. Ask your admin which role you are assigned.
